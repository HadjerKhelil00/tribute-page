Who is Isaac Newton?

Isaac Newton was a highly influential English physicist, mathematician, and astronomer who is famous for his laws of motion and universal gravitation. He was born in Woolsthorpe, England, and showed an early aptitude for mathematics and science. He studied at Trinity College, Cambridge, where he developed his theories on calculus, optics, and mechanics. In 1687, he published his seminal work "Mathematical Principles of Natural Philosophy" (also known as the "Principia"), which included his laws of motion and the law of universal gravitation. Newton's work had a profound impact on the development of modern science, and his ideas continue to influence our understanding of the physical world today.
